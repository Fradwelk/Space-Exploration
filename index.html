Space Exploration

The universe contains everything around us. It is so large that our minds cannot even begin to comprehend its sheer size and magnitude. To better understand the universe, we have arranged it in units like galaxies, clusters, and superclusters, much like how a body is made up of trillions of cells. Let us take a look at all the weird, exciting, and interesting things our universe has in store for us. But before we explore, let us first take a look at some of the tools and equipment we use to observe and discover new parts of the massive universe we live in.





Name: Hubble Space Telescope

Description:

The Hubble Space Telescope is one of our most precious tools in exploring the universe. It has taken countless amazing pictures of the Universe




Name: International Space Station

Description:

The ISS is the biggest artifical satelite orbiting the Earth. Aboard it, we can find scientists, engineers, and astrophysicists performing experiments and observations to better understand our universe and its contents.




Name: James Webb Telescope

Description:

The James Webb Telescope is a massive improvement from the Hubble Telescope. While the Hubble Telescope is very impressive, it is not able to take high-resolution images of far away subjects.




Our Solar System

Our solar system is unique from all the ones we've observed. This is for one reason, there is life found within it. While there are potentially billions of planets in the universe capable of supporting life, we have only ever found it on Earth. Our solar system also features one of the most unique planets in the form of Saturn, which is very well known for possessing a ring of debris around it, giving it a beautiful appearance.

Super Clusters

Superclusters are the biggest celestial bodies in the universe. They are made up of galaxy clusters, meaning they contain millions, maybe even billions of galaxies within them. They span massive distances and can be billions of light years in size. There are an estimated 10 million Superclusters that make up the observable universe.




Name: The Virgo Supercluster

Number of Galaxy Clusters: ~100

Size: 110 Million Light Years




Name: The Saraswati Supercluster

Number of Galaxy Clusters: 43

Size: 326.19 Million Light Years




Name: Hercules–Corona Borealis Great Wall (Largest Object in the Universe)

Number of Galaxy Clusters: Unkown due to sheer size and distance from Earth

Size: 10 Billion Light Years (The universe itself is only around 93 Billion Light Years!)
